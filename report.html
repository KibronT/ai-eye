Research Report · 2025

AI vs. Human: How to Tell the Difference

A linguistic analysis of AI-generated versus human-written news articles - exploring patterns that can help readers spot the difference.

Created by Sprintern Cohort 2025 · University of Maryland

Project Overview

What Did We Research?

This report investigates a growing challenge in the digital age: how can readers tell the difference between news articles written by humans and those generated by AI?

The research team used Python-based natural language processing to analyze a set of news articles - comparing those written by journalists with those produced by AI tools. The analysis examined several linguistic features across each article.

The goal was not just academic. The team aimed to surface practical tips that everyday readers can use to be more critical and informed consumers of online content.

Research AI Literacy Winter 2025

AI-Generated Articles

AI news articles can hallucinate facts, produce plausible-sounding but incorrect information, and may mislead readers who do not check sources carefully.

Our Analysis Method

Python was used to measure emotional variation, sentence length, use of named entities (people, places, dates), repetition rates, and transitional language across both sets of articles.

Key Findings

  • Human-written articles tend to be more emotionally neutral overall
  • Human articles mention specific dates, people, and organizations significantly more often
  • Human articles use more complex sentence structures and varied vocabulary
  • AI articles often use more transitional phrases and filler language

Takeaways for Readers

Practical Tips for Spotting AI Content

Look for Specific Details

Human writers tend to include specific dates, names, and organizations. Vague or missing details can be a sign of AI generation.

Notice Repetitive Phrasing

AI often repeats the same transitions and phrases throughout an article. Watch for formulaic structure that feels copy-pasted.

Check the Sources

AI can cite sources that do not exist or misrepresent real sources. Always verify important claims using a trusted search engine.
