What to Watch For

Common Types of AI Scams

Scammers are using AI in new ways. Knowing what to look for is your first line of defense.

AI Voice Cloning

Scammers can clone a person's voice using just a few seconds of audio from social media. They call family members pretending to be someone in distress, often claiming to need money urgently.

Warning sign: Urgent requests for money, even from a familiar voice

Deepfake Videos

AI can create realistic fake videos of real people, including politicians, celebrities, or family members, saying things they never actually said. These are used to spread false information or manipulate people.

Warning sign: Video content that seems off or surprising from a trusted figure

AI-Written Phishing Messages

AI can now write highly convincing emails, texts, and messages that impersonate banks, government agencies, or companies. These are harder to spot than older scams because they have fewer spelling and grammar errors.

Warning sign: Unsolicited messages asking for personal information or passwords

Protect Yourself

Safety Tips

Pause Before Responding

If a message or call creates urgency or panic, that is often intentional. Slow down, breathe, and verify before taking any action.

Call Back on a Known Number

If someone calls claiming to be a family member or an organization, hang up and call them back at a number you already know is real.

Never Share Personal Info Unexpectedly

No legitimate organization will ask for passwords, Social Security numbers, or bank details unexpectedly by phone, text, or email.
